Project Sprout

The sustainability of design is becoming an increasing concern in architecture. Whilst there are many methodologies to address it, and standards to aim for, quantitative methods for analysing a buildings impact are still relatively new and are yet to penetrate the design process. Typically this would be outsourced to an external consultant, or if it wasn’t a priority, would be avoided.

Some information has been omitted from this case study due to confidentiality. The views and information shown are based of my own opinions and findings, which are not necessarily representative of Foster+Partners

My Role

During my time at Foster + Partners (F+P), I worked as a material researcher collaborating with a range of teams across the practice, addressing architectural material issues.

Whilst in this position I worked with all kinds of disciplines and saw opportunities to streamline our workload and improve the way myself and my colleagues worked.

So using my design knowledge, and programming skills I was teaching myself in my own time, I put together a range of tools to be used by the employees of F+P.

EPD

EPDs (or Environmental Product Declarations) are independently verified reports conducted to ISO standards in order to evaluate a supply chains sustainability. They look at a range of environmental impacts across the lifecycle of a product in order to measure the sustainability of it.

However EPDs are technical documents, they aren't friendly to non-technically experienced users and can be difficult to understand

100 EPD's were examined, and the following issues were found:

  • The level of detail and length varied from 6 to 22 pages
  • There were inconsistencies in the functional unit applied for the calculations e.g. some would have a panel as a unit, others would have 1m3 of material
  • There was no visual consistency between EPD’s
  • There are a number of technical terms and acronyms that are rarely explained


Ultimately this meant that the important information wasn't accessible to architects

So how can we change it?
